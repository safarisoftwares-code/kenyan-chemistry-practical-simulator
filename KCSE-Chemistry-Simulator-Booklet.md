1. ABOUT THIS SOFTWARE

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The Kenyan Chemistry Practical Simulator is an interactive 
educational tool designed for secondary school students 
preparing for the KCSE Chemistry Paper 3 (Practical) exam.

It simulates common laboratory experiments including:
• Ion reactions with test tube visualization
• Flame tests with Bunsen burner animation
• Acid-base titration with burette readings
• Gas identification tests
• Cation confirmatory tests with NaOH and NH₃
• Enthalpy experiments with temperature-time graphs
• Practice questions with auto-marking

The software supports both WORD EQUATIONS (for Form 1-2 
students) and IONIC EQUATIONS (for Form 3-4 students).

8. TAB 3: TITRATION SIMULATOR

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Purpose:
Simulate acid-base titration with burette readings, indicator
colour changes, and auto-calculations.

How to Use:
1. Select an experiment from the dropdown (5 options)
2. Tap the blue valve (💧) to release acid dropwise
3. Watch the indicator colour change at endpoint
4. Record each titre using "📝 Record Titre"
5. After 4 titres, view the average calculation

Experiments Available:
1. HCl vs NaOH (phenolphthalein)
2. H₂SO₄ vs NaOH (methyl orange)
3. HCl vs Na₂CO₃ (methyl orange)
4. H₂SO₄ vs KOH (phenolphthalein)
5. HCl vs NaHCO₃ (methyl orange)

Key Features:
• 25.0 cm³ pipette for base
• Flask never exceeds 60% capacity
• Endpoint flashes "🎯 ENDPOINT!"
• First titre is the rough titre (omitted from average)
• Calculations shown step by step

Practice Calculations:
1. Write the balanced equation
2. Calculate average titre (omit first rough titre)
3. Calculate moles of base used
4. Use mole ratio to find moles of acid
5. Calculate molarity of the acid

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Purpose:
Simulate temperature changes during chemical reactions and
plot temperature-time graphs.

How to Use:
1. Select an experiment from the dropdown
2. Click "▶ Start" to begin recording
3. Observe temperature changes every 30 seconds
4. Watch the thermometer and graph update
5. View ΔH calculation results after 3 minutes

Experiments Available:
1. NaOH + HCl - Neutralization (plateau behaviour)
2. Zn + CuSO₄ - Displacement (peak then slight fall)
3. NH₄NO₃ + H₂O - Dissolving (steady drop)

Data Recorded:
• Time (0s to 180s at 30s intervals)
• Temperature readings
• SVG graph of Temperature vs Time
• ΔT calculation
• ΔH value in kJ/mol

Practice Questions:
• Why does temperature plateau?
• Why might temperature fall after a peak?
• Calculate q = mcΔT
• Why use a polystyrene cup?
• Is the reaction endothermic or exothermic?

Purpose:
Comprehensive reference of common precipitates formed in
KCSE practical exams, with ionic equations and colours.

Sections:
🟡 CARBONATES (CO₃²⁻)
   Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺, Zn²⁺, Fe²⁺, Cu²⁺, Pb²⁺, Ba²⁺
   All form precipitates except Na⁺, K⁺, NH₄⁺

🟠 SULPHATES (SO₄²⁻)
   Ca²⁺ (sparingly), Ba²⁺, Pb²⁺
   All others soluble

⚪ CHLORIDES (Cl⁻)
   Pb²⁺ (soluble in hot water), Ag⁺
   All others soluble

🔵 HYDROXIDES (OH⁻)
   Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺, Al³⁺, Zn²⁺, Fe²⁺, Fe³⁺, Cu²⁺, Pb²⁺
   Al³⁺, Zn²⁺, Pb²⁺ dissolve in excess NaOH
   Na⁺, K⁺, NH₄⁺, Ba²⁺ hydroxides are soluble

🟤 BROMIDES & IODIDES
   PbBr₂ (cream), PbI₂ (bright yellow), CuI + I₂ (brown)

🟢 SULPHITES (SO₃²⁻)
   CaSO₃ (white), BaSO₃ (white)

🔄 COMPLEX IONS & EXCESS REAGENT
   Al(OH)₃ + NaOH → Na[Al(OH)₄]
   Zn(OH)₂ + NaOH → Na₂[Zn(OH)₄]
   Pb(OH)₂ + NaOH → Na₂[Pb(OH)₄]
   Cu(OH)₂ + NH₃ → [Cu(NH₃)₄]²⁺
   AgCl + NH₃ → [Ag(NH₃)₂]⁺

KCSE SOLUBILITY RULES:
✅ Always soluble: Na⁺, K⁺, NH₄⁺ salts
✅ All nitrates (NO₃⁻) are soluble
✅ Most chlorides soluble (except Pb²⁺, Ag⁺)
❌ Most carbonates are insoluble
❌ BaSO₄, PbSO₄, CaSO₄ are insoluble
❌ Most hydroxides insoluble (except Na⁺, K⁺, NH₄⁺, Ba²⁺)

Essential KCSE Radicals:
1. MnO₄⁻ - Purple, oxidizing agent, redox titrations
2. Cr₂O₇²⁻ - Orange→green when reduced (SO₂ test)
3. S₂O₃²⁻ - Iodine titration ("clock reaction")
4. SO₄²⁻ - White ppt with Ba²⁺ (sulphate test)
5. CO₃²⁻ - Produces CO₂ with acids

Oxidation State Rules:
1. Sum in neutral compound = 0
2. Sum in ion = charge on ion
3. Oxygen = -2 (except peroxides: -1)
4. Hydrogen = +1 (except metal hydrides: -1)
5. Group 1 metals = +1, Group 2 = +2
